# SEC filing research checklist

Research education only. Not financial advice.

## 1. Company basics

- Ticker and company name
- Filing type and date
- Business description
- Segments and revenue streams
- Customer or supplier concentration

## 2. What changed

Compare the latest filing with the prior comparable period.

- Revenue
- Gross margin
- Operating income or loss
- Cash and equivalents
- Debt and maturities
- Operating cash flow
- Share count
- Segment results
- New or intensified risk language

## 3. Liquidity and dilution

Check the filing for:

- Going concern language
- Shelf registration
- ATM offering program
- Warrants
- Convertibles
- Recent private placements
- Covenant issues
- Management comments on funding needs

## 4. Risk factor triage

Rank risks by directness to:

- Revenue
- Regulation
- Financing
- Survival
- Customer concentration
- Product dependency
- Legal exposure

Mark each risk as generic, company-specific, or newly intensified.

## 5. Source trail requirements

For every claim, preserve:

- Source type
- Filing or publication date
- Exact excerpt or table row
- Why it matters
- Confidence
- Unknowns
